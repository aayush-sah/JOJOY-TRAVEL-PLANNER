Athirappilly Waterfalls, often referred to as the "Niagara of India," is the largest waterfall in Kerala, India. Located on the Chalakudy River in the Thrissur district, it stands at 81.5 feet (25 meters) tall and is renowned for its breathtaking beauty and the lush greenery that surrounds it. The falls are situated near the Sholayar ranges and are a popular destination for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts.

Entry Fee: As of December 2024, the entry fee for Indian nationals is ₹30 per person.

Facilities: The area offers a well-maintained pathway leading to the base of the falls, allowing visitors to experience the full grandeur of the waterfall. There are also designated viewpoints for photography and relaxation.

Average Cost to Visit:

The cost of visiting Athirappilly Waterfalls can vary based on your travel preferences and the duration of your stay. Here's a general breakdown:

Transportation: If you're traveling from Malhanwara, Madhya Pradesh, to Athirappilly, the most convenient option is to fly to Cochin International Airport, approximately 55 kilometers away. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or use local transportation to reach the falls. The cost of a one-way taxi ride from the airport to Athirappilly can range from ₹1,500 to ₹2,500, depending on the type of vehicle and negotiation.

Accommodation: There are several hotels and resorts near Athirappilly catering to different budgets. On average, a mid-range hotel room can cost between ₹2,000 to ₹5,000 per night.

Meals: Dining at local restaurants can cost approximately ₹500 to ₹1,000 per person per day, depending on your choice of eateries.

Entry Fee: As mentioned earlier, the entry fee to the falls is ₹30 per person.
